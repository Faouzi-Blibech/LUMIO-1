## Problem Statement

**Core gap:** Tunisian primary and middle school students with ADHD have no coordinated real-time tool connecting student + teacher + parent.

**Key statistics (use in pitch):**
- ADHD affects 7.6% of children (3–12) and 5.6% of adolescents (12–18) globally
- Tunisia Monastir (2022): 18.1% high school students show ADHD symptoms
- Tunisia Sfax (2021): 9.94% of primary school children affected
- 72.54% of affected Tunisian children have ≥1 comorbid condition
- 33.2% of affected students fail to graduate on time
- Only 32% receive any behavioral classroom support
- Only 31% of parents receive any behavioral guidance

## Four User Roles

The platform serves **four** distinct user roles:

1. **Student** — Study sessions with timer, dashboard, homework, classes, AI companion
2. **Teacher** — Class management, student monitoring (table with hover/click), homework, settings, AI assistant
3. **Parent** — Child's progress dashboard, homework consultation, settings, AI assistant
4. **Admin** — School-wide statistics dashboard, teacher/class management table, settings, AI assistant
